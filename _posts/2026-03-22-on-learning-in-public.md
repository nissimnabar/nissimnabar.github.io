One of the best ways to learn something is to write about it. Not because you're an expert, but precisely because you're not.

When you write, you're forced to confront the gaps in your understanding. You can't hand-wave through a blog post the way you can in your own head. The act of explaining clarifies thinking.

## Why it works

There's a concept called the Feynman Technique — explain something in simple terms, identify where you get stuck, go back and fill the gaps. Writing is that loop, externalized.

## The fear

Most people don't write because they're afraid of being wrong. But being wrong in public, and then correcting yourself, is one of the fastest ways to learn. The feedback loop is real and immediate.

So here's to learning in public. More posts to come.
